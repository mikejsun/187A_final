Stores

Websites selected for your project needs. Currently browsing for: ALL STORES
  • All Stores

Finding specialized stores for your project can be a hassle. Here's a list of stores that are specialized in not only microprocessors, but specifically geared for your Arduino project needs. They also have generous variety and pricetags!

Sparkfun

Location: Boulder, Colorado
Inventory Size: Large
Video Instructions: Yes
View online store: Sparkfun.com

Description: The undisputed king of hobbyist electronics suppliers, Sparkfun has remained true to its DIY roots even as its size and offerings expand. As they explain themselves, their site is about making electronics more accessible to the average person. They may not always be the cheapest, but their prices are fair and their staff is friendly and knowleagable.

Seeedstudio

Location: Shenzhen, China
Inventory Size: Large
Video Instructions: No
View online store: Seeedstudio.com

Description: Seeedstudio is an open hardware facilitation company based in Shenzhen, China. I've found this place to have one of the bigger selections for your Arduino projects and is a very suitable source for both beginners and advanced innovators. Just as a heads up though: shipping costs will most likely neutralize your bill.

Maker Shed

Location: Sebastopol, CA
Inventory Size: Medium
Video instructions: Yes
View online store: Makershed.com

Description: Launched originally as a source for back issues of MAKE Magazine, the Maker Shed expanded rapidly to meet the demand for 'projects in a box,' otherwise known as kits. Love their video instructions, too.

Adafruit

Location: New York, NY
Inventory Size: Medium
Video Instructions: No
View online store: Adafruit.com

Description: Adafruit is all about open source hardware and good old-fashioned hacking. Like Sparkfun, they have their own line of custom Arduino shields and specialize in providing hard-to-find prototyping parts and accessories. 1There are some heavy hitters that have a huge presence in all aspects of the Arduino community, this is one of them.

Open Electronics

Location: Italy
Inventory Size: Small
Video Instructions: No
View online store: Open-electronics.org

Description: Open-electronics.org is an open source reference site with ideas and feedback aimed to enrich the community. It's also a site built by engineers who do Arduino-related projects. Their inventory is limited, but worth the visit.
