    • Here's a list of websites that will help you get started with Arduino. Please note the level of difficulty for each and plan accordingly.

Arduino.cc

Platform: Arduino
Difficulty: Beginner
Source: Arduino.cc

Description: Arduino is an open-source electronics prototyping platform based on flexible, easy-to-use hardware and software. It's intended for artists, designers, hobbyists, and anyone interested in creating interactive objects or environments. Arduino can sense the environment by receiving input from a variety of sensors and can affect its surroundings by controlling lights, motors, and other actuators.

Arduino meets Processing

Platform: Arduino & Processing
Difficulty: Intermediate
View page: ArduinomeetsProcessing

Description:: The Arduino meets Processing project intends to make it as easy as possible for anyone to explore the world of physical computing. All you need is an Arduino board as well as the Arduino and Processing software, which you can download on their project websites.

Ladyada.net

Platform: Arduino
Difficulty: Intermediate
View page: Ladyada.net

Description:: Ladyada hosts a short tutorial course that is excellent for jumping into Arduino and electronics for the first time.

Freeduino.org

Platform: Arduino
Difficulty: Intermediate
View page: Freeduino.org

Description:: Freeduino.org offers a HUGE list of tutorials, example projects, and articles with general info and best practices. Tough to get through them all, but it's in alphabetical order and worth a check if you're having trouble finding info elsewhere.

Tronixstuff.wordpress.com

Platform: Arduino
Difficulty: Advanced
View page: Tronixstuff.wordpress.com

Description:: Tronixstuff has a long series of Arduino tutorials that begin with the basics and move all the way through advanced sensing and communications. The material there is enough to be a thorough book all on its own.
